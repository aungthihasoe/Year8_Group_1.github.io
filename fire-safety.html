Fire Safety

Prevent house fires before they start.

Fire safety starts with small habits at home. This page gives simple steps for prevention, home preparation, escape planning, and what to do fast if a fire happens.

Prevent Prepare Protect
ACT FAST!
If you smell smoke or hear an alarm, warn everyone and move out immediately.

How to Prevent House Fires

Small routines make a big difference.

Use these prevention habits every day to lower fire risk in bedrooms, kitchens, living rooms, and anywhere power or heat is used.

01

Heating Safety

Keep space heaters at least three feet away from curtains, furniture, beds, and anything that can burn. Turn them off before leaving the room.

02

Smoking Safety

Never smoke near anything flammable. Make sure cigarette butts are fully extinguished with water before throwing them away.

03

Child Safety

Store matches and lighters in a high, locked cabinet where children cannot reach them.

04

Electrical Care

Do not overload power strips or extension cords. Keep water away from electrical devices and damaged wires.

05

Kitchen Safety

Never leave cooking unattended. Keep dish towels, oven mitts, paper, and other flammable items away from the stove area.

Preparation at Home

Be ready before an emergency starts.

Preparation helps your family move faster, stay calmer, and avoid confusion during a real fire emergency.

Fire Equipment

  • Keep a fire extinguisher in the kitchen, living room, and bedroom.
  • Make sure people in the home know how to use it.

Install Smoke Alarms

  • Place alarms on every level of your home.
  • Test them monthly and replace batteries at least once a year.

Emergency Kit

  • Prepare spare batteries and a flashlight.
  • Add a first-aid kit, some cash, important documents, and medicines you need.

Escape Plan

  • Identify two ways out of your house.
  • Choose a safe meeting place outside at a safe distance from home.

What to Do If a Fire Happens

React fast and get everyone out.

These are the most important actions during a fire. They should be quick, simple, and easy to remember.

ACT FAST!
If a fire alarm is active or you smell smoke, shout "FIRE" to alert everyone in the house immediately.
01

Stay Low

Smoke is more dangerous than flames. If there is smoke in front of you, crawl on your hands and knees where the air is cleaner.

02

Check Doors

Before opening a door, feel it with the back of your hand. If it is hot, do not open it and use your secondary escape plan.

03

Get Out and Stay Out

Once you are outside, go directly to your planned safe place. Never go back inside for pets, belongings, or other people.

04

Call 191

Call the Myanmar Fire Department at 191 or your local fire department station once you are in a safe place.

Emergency Actions

Remember: SDR

If clothes catch fire, use this quick action routine immediately.

S

Stop

STOP where you are.

D

Drop

DROP to the ground and cover your face with your hands.

R

Roll

ROLL over and over to smother the flames.

Stay calm, get out, and call for help.

Fire safety is not about memorizing many rules. It is about a few strong habits: spot risks early, know your exits, stay low in smoke, and never go back into a burning place.
