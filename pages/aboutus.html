How to Use Crisis Compass

A simple workflow you can follow anytime.

1. Pick a topic

  • Choose a disaster type or safety topic
  • Start with the most common risks

2. Learn the steps

  • Read the before, during, and after actions
  • Save the key points you will remember

3. Make a plan

  • Write emergency contacts
  • Set a meeting point for your family
  • Practice small habits monthly

Why This Project Matters

Good safety guides should feel human, not confusing.

We made this website because many safety pages feel too long or too hard to read in a stressful moment. Our goal is to keep the advice simple, useful, and easy to share with family, classmates, and neighbors.

FAQ

Short answers, no confusion.

Is Crisis Compass an emergency service?

No. It is an educational project. In urgent situations, contact official emergency services.

Can I trust everything here?

We focus on basic, widely taught safety actions. Always follow local authorities during real events.

What should I do first?

Start by saving key contacts and learning the before, during, and after steps for common hazards.

Keep safety simple

Learn one topic today and share it with your family. Small actions done early help a lot later.
