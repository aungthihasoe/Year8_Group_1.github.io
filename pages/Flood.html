Flood Information

Learn what floods are, common causes, and what families and neighbourhoods can do before, during, and after rising water.

Read Safety Tips Emergency Kit

Quick Definition

A flood is when water covers land that is normally dry. It can come from heavy rain, overflowing rivers, storm surge, or dam failure.

Floods can move fast — a simple plan and a small kit saves time.

About Floods

Flooding happens when water covers land that is normally dry. Floods can damage homes, roads, and crops, and create dangerous conditions like strong currents and contaminated water.

Heavy Rain

Intense or long rain can overwhelm drains and rivers.

River Overflow

Rivers can spill their banks after heavy upstream rain.

Coastal Surge

Storm surges push sea water inland during storms.

Flood Guide

What causes floods?

Floods come from heavy rain, overflowing rivers, storms, or dam failures. When the ground can't absorb water, it spreads across land that is usually dry. Floods can be sudden (flash floods) or slow and long-lasting.

Why prepare?

Floods can rise quickly. Preparation helps you get to safety early, avoid injury, and reduce damage. A simple plan helps people stay calm and act faster.

How to prepare

  • Keep important documents in waterproof bags
  • Store valuables and supplies on higher levels
  • Prepare a kit: water, food, torch, batteries, first aid
  • Know evacuation routes and nearby high ground
  • Watch weather forecasts and flood warnings

What to do immediately

  • Move to higher ground right away
  • Follow evacuation orders from officials
  • Do not walk, swim, or drive through floodwater
  • Keep away from rivers, drains, and fast-flowing water

Negative consequences

  • Drowning and serious injuries
  • Damage to homes, roads, and buildings
  • Contaminated water that spreads disease
  • Loss of power and clean water supply

After a flood

  • Avoid floodwater — it may be contaminated
  • Stay away from damaged electrical wires and buildings
  • Return home only when authorities say it is safe
  • Clean and disinfect your home and belongings

Tips & Tricks

Leave early if water levels start rising
Never underestimate floodwater—even shallow water can be dangerous
Wear strong shoes to protect your feet from sharp objects
Keep a waterproof emergency bag ready at all times

Common Flood Dangers

Strong Currents

Even shallow moving water can knock people off their feet.

Contamination

Floodwater can contain sewage, chemicals, and sharp objects.

Hidden Damage

Water can weaken floors and walls; use caution when returning home.
