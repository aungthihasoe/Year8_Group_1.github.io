Quick Guide

Disaster Safety Tips

Use these quick reminders for different emergencies. They are short on purpose, so they are easier to remember.

Flood Safety Tips +

Before a Flood

  • Prepare an emergency kit with food, water, flashlights, and medicine.
  • Keep important documents in waterproof bags.
  • Know the evacuation routes in your area.

During a Flood

  • Move to higher ground immediately.
  • Avoid walking or driving through flood water.
  • Turn off electricity if instructed to do so.

After a Flood

  • Stay away from damaged buildings and power lines.
  • Boil water before drinking if the supply may be contaminated.
  • Clean and disinfect flooded areas carefully.
Tsunami Safety Tips +

Before a Tsunami

  • Learn warning signs such as strong earthquakes or sudden sea withdrawal.
  • Prepare an emergency bag and evacuation plan.
  • Know nearby safe zones and higher ground locations.

During a Tsunami

  • Evacuate immediately after a tsunami warning.
  • Move inland or to higher ground as quickly as possible.
  • Stay away from beaches and coastal areas.

After a Tsunami

  • Wait for official announcements before returning.
  • Be careful of floodwater and debris.
  • Check for injuries and help others if safe.
Fire Safety Tips +

Before a Fire

  • Install smoke alarms and check them regularly.
  • Keep flammable materials away from heat sources.
  • Create a family fire escape plan.

During a Fire

  • Stay low to avoid smoke inhalation.
  • Use stairs instead of elevators.
  • Call emergency services once you are safe.

After a Fire

  • Do not re-enter damaged buildings until authorities allow it.
  • Watch for hot spots or weakened structures.
  • Seek medical attention if exposed to smoke.
Tornado Safety Tips +

Before a Tornado

  • Monitor weather updates and emergency alerts.
  • Identify a safe shelter such as an interior room.
  • Prepare emergency supplies and batteries.

During a Tornado

  • Take shelter immediately in a small windowless room.
  • Protect your head and neck with your arms or a pillow.
  • Avoid staying inside vehicles or mobile homes.

After a Tornado

  • Watch out for broken glass and fallen power lines.
  • Help injured people if it is safe.
  • Continue listening to emergency instructions.
Earthquake Safety Tips +

Before an Earthquake

  • Secure heavy furniture and appliances properly.
  • Prepare an emergency supply kit.
  • Practice earthquake drills with family members.

During an Earthquake

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On.
  • Stay away from windows and heavy objects.
  • If outdoors, move to an open area away from buildings.

After an Earthquake

  • Check for injuries and damaged utilities.
  • Be prepared for aftershocks.
  • Follow emergency updates and evacuation orders.
