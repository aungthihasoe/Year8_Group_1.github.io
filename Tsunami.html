Tsunami Information

A tsunami is a series of large waves caused by sudden movement on the sea floor. Know the signs and what to do if one happens near your area.

Read Safety Tips Emergency Kit

Quick Definition

A tsunami is a series of fast ocean waves caused by sudden seabed movement. They can travel a long way and push water far inland.

If you feel strong shaking near the coast, move inland and uphill right away.

About Tsunamis

A tsunami can travel far inland depending on its size and local geography. They are rare but dangerous; fast evacuation to higher ground is essential.

Causes

Often triggered by undersea earthquakes, landslides, or eruptions.

Warning Signs

Strong shaking, sudden sea retreat, or loud ocean noise.

Speed

Tsunami waves can travel very fast across deep water.

Safety Tips

Before a Tsunami

  • Know evacuation routes and nearby high ground.
  • Practice quick exits from low-lying areas with your family.
  • Keep a small emergency kit in case roads are blocked.

During a Tsunami

  • If you feel strong shaking, move inland and uphill immediately.
  • Do not go to the shore to watch — waves can come in series and grow larger.
  • Follow official orders and stay away until it is safe.

After a Tsunami

  • Keep away from flooded or damaged areas until authorities say it's safe.
  • Report hazards and help neighbours who may need assistance.
  • Expect a long recovery period — be patient and follow official guidance.
